The problem

The industry routes denied claims to humans. Almvia executes the response.

When a physician orders a procedure, someone on your team has to get insurance approval. They dig through notes, outside records, and payer policies — for every single request. Thirteen hours a week, per physician.

Then the insurer says no. Most practices write a generic response that rarely wins, or write off the revenue entirely because chasing it takes too long.

Current tools organize the queue. They don't do the work. Almvia does. It reads the denial reason, retrieves evidence from your EHR via FHIR, cross-references the payer's criteria, and generates a cited appeal narrative. Your staff reviews and submits. The hunting is done.

How it works

Four steps.
Fully automated.

From denial to appeal-ready packet, without your staff touching the chart.

01
Denial detected

Almvia reads the denial code and identifies the exact medical necessity criterion the payer applied. Under the CMS final rule effective 2027, payers must give specific denial reasons. That specificity is the input.

02
Chart retrieved

Almvia connects to your EHR via FHIR R4 and pulls the full clinical record — diagnoses, meds, labs, encounters. It simultaneously ingests unstructured sources: faxed consults, scanned imaging, and outside records the EHR doesn't capture.

03
Policy matched

Evidence is cross-referenced against the payer's medical necessity policy for that procedure. Almvia identifies what supports the case, flags gaps, and cites every finding with source, date, and page.

04
Appeal drafted

A cited appeal narrative lands in your team's inbox. They review, edit if needed, and approve. Nothing ships without sign-off. Every action is logged.

Prior authorization

Built before the denial arrives.

Most denials are preventable at submission. Almvia monitors your EHR in real time, detects procedures that need prior auth, pulls the clinical evidence, checks it against payer criteria, and flags what's missing before the packet goes out.

What Almvia builds for every prior auth request

  • A clinical summary tying diagnosis to procedure and payer criteria.
  • A cited evidence list — notes, labs, imaging, and history, each linked to source and date.
  • A missing items checklist flagging what the payer will ask for before you submit.
  • A portal-ready bundle formatted for submission.

Get it right the first time. Every denial avoided is hours of rework your team doesn't touch.

Denials and appeals

When the denial arrives, the work is already done.

A denial used to start a long manual process. Almvia makes it the start of an automated one.

When a denial arrives — fax, PDF, or remit file — Almvia catches it immediately, reads the specific denial reason required under federal rules, and identifies the exact criterion the payer says wasn't met.

It then retrieves the chart evidence that addresses that criterion directly. Not a general patient summary — the specific documentation that responds to the specific reason, cited to source, date, and page.

The appeal narrative addresses the payer's criterion point by point, structured in the format their process requires. Your staff reviews, edits, and approves. Nothing leaves without sign-off. Full audit log on every action.

Almvia tracks every appeal through resolution. Outcomes are logged — approved, escalated, overturned. That data improves every future submission.

Built on what the regulations now require

CMS now requires payers to give specific denial reasons and respond to prior auth requests via FHIR API. Almvia is the provider-side system built to act on both.

CMS-0057-F · 21st Century Cures Act · FHIR R4 Native

The CMS final rule (January 2024) requires payers to implement FHIR R4 APIs for prior auth by 2027 and give a specific reason for every denial. The Cures Act prohibits EHR information blocking — the legal foundation for Almvia's integration. CAQH estimates full electronic prior auth adoption saves $515 million annually.
