The problem

The industry assigns
denied claims to humans.
We assign them to Almvia.

When a physician orders a procedure, someone on your admin team has to get insurance approval first. They open the EHR, hunt through notes and outside records, interpret the payer's medical policy, and package it all up. For every single request. Then the insurer says no, and the whole cycle starts over for the appeal.

Current tools make that queue more organized. They don't do the work. Almvia does the work.

When a claim gets denied, Almvia reads the specific denial reason now required under CMS's 2024 prior authorization final rule, pulls the relevant chart evidence via your EHR's FHIR API, cross-references the payer's published medical policy, and generates a fully cited appeal narrative. Your staff reviews, approves, and submits. The clinical legwork is already done.

How it works

Four steps.
Fully automated.

01
Denial detected

Almvia reads the denial code and identifies the specific medical necessity criterion the payer applied. Under CMS-0057-F, payers now have to provide specific denial reasons instead of vague rejections. That specificity is the input.

02
Chart retrieved

Almvia queries your EHR via FHIR R4 for the patient's clinical record (diagnoses, medications, labs, prior encounters) and simultaneously ingests unstructured sources like faxed consults, scanned imaging reports, and outside records.

03
Policy matched

The retrieved evidence gets cross-referenced against the payer's published medical necessity policy for that specific procedure. Almvia identifies what supports the case, what's missing, and cites every finding with source, date, and page.

04
Appeal drafted

A fully cited appeal narrative lands in your admin team's inbox, ready to review. They verify, tweak if needed, and approve. Nothing gets submitted without explicit sign-off. The clinical work is already done.

Built on what the regulations now require

CMS now requires payers to give specific denial reasons and respond to prior auth requests via FHIR API. Almvia is the provider-side system built to act on both.
